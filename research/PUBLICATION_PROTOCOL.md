# Locked publication protocol: physics-prior credibility for bearing RUL

Status: version 0.1 evaluated in EXP-007; gate failed; amendment required before new experiments

Protocol version: 0.1

Date: 21 July 2026

Working method name: **PriorCred-RUL** (temporary; not a publication claim)

## 1. Research objective

The project will test whether the applicability of a bearing-degradation prior can be
estimated from information available at prediction time and used to prevent physics-induced
negative transfer in remaining useful life prediction.

The primary objective is not to build another attention architecture or to force predictions
to follow a chosen law. It is to decide when strong physics is sufficiently supported to be
used and when the model should fall back to weak priors or data-only prediction.

## 2. Research questions and hypotheses

### RQ1: Can prior misspecification be detected under known truth?

**H1.** A label-safe credibility estimator will distinguish valid from deliberately corrupted
degradation priors on controlled synthetic trajectories better than chance and will be
calibrated enough for a predeclared acceptance threshold.

### RQ2: Does credibility control prevent negative transfer?

**H2.** Credibility-weighted physics will reduce positive physics regret relative to a fixed
strong PINN and generic adaptive-weight baselines on held-out trajectories and conditions.
Physics regret for fold (j) is:

\[
R^{\mathrm{phys}}_j = \mathrm{RMSE}^{\mathrm{physics}}_j
                     - \mathrm{RMSE}^{\mathrm{data\ only}}_j.
\]

Positive values mean the physics intervention harmed prediction.

### RQ3: Does the diagnostic remain useful on real bearings?

**H3.** Without using test RUL labels, lower estimated credibility will be associated with
larger realized physics regret across held-out real bearings, lifecycle regions, and operating
conditions. This is an external diagnostic claim, not proof that the synthetic and real
physical mechanisms are identical.

### RQ4: Is any benefit independent of input representation?

**H4.** The direction of the primary findings will survive one frozen raw-signal or hybrid
representation confirmation after the feature-based method study is complete.

## 3. What PriorCred-RUL is and is not

For candidate prior (k), trajectory (i), and prediction time (t), define an applicability
or credibility value (c_{i,t,k}\in[0,1]). It is not the predictive confidence interval and it
is not simply an inverse physics residual. It estimates whether the assumptions needed by
prior (k) are supported in the observed context.

The final objective has the conceptual form:

\[
\mathcal L = \mathcal L_{\mathrm{RUL}}
  + \lambda_{\mathrm{weak}}\mathcal L_{\mathrm{weak}}
  + \sum_k \lambda_k\,m_{i,t,k}\,
    \operatorname{stopgrad}(c_{i,t,k})\,\mathcal L_{\mathrm{phys},k},
\]

where (m_{i,t,k}) is a sensor/parameter availability mask. If every strong-prior
credibility is low, the method must reduce to the same data-only backbone plus only the
predeclared weak constraints. It may not invent missing temperature, load, lubricant, crack,
or material measurements.

### 3.1 Credibility evidence allowed at prediction time

Candidate inputs to the credibility estimator are:

- standardized residual evidence generated by a frozen, cross-fitted data-only trajectory;
- uncertainty of physics-parameter identification from training/source data;
- disagreement among plausible physics-parameter or law ensembles;
- label-free covariate or representation shift relative to the training population;
- operating condition and lifecycle-position proxies available up to time (t);
- sensor/parameter availability and missingness; and
- physically meaningful range or identifiability diagnostics.

The estimator may not use target-test RUL, failure time, total trajectory length, future
observations, test metrics, or a statistic fitted on the test labels.

### 3.2 Anti-collapse requirements

An end-to-end gate can minimize the training objective by setting every credibility to zero.
That solution is forbidden. The first implementation must therefore:

1. fit a data-only backbone on training units only;
2. generate credibility features by cross-fitting within the training population;
3. train/calibrate the credibility estimator using synthetic known-validity labels and
   source-validation diagnostics, separately from the final RUL objective;
4. freeze or stop the gradient through credibility when it weights physics losses;
5. retain explicit all-zero, all-one, random, and oracle credibility controls; and
6. report the credibility distribution and fallback rate, so silent gate collapse is visible.

A later jointly trained variant is an ablation, not the primary method, until it demonstrates
that it cannot exploit labels or collapse.

## 4. Candidate priors and applicability limits

| Prior | Status | What it can legitimately constrain | Required evidence and limits |
|---|---|---|---|
| RUL/degradation monotonicity | Weak structural prior | Penalize local health recovery or increasing damage | Allows noise-tolerant violations. It is not a crack-growth law. |
| Positivity and failure boundary | Weak structural prior | Nonnegative RUL/damage and declared terminal boundary | The boundary must follow the dataset failure definition, not an arbitrary target fitted from test data. |
| Synthetic generator progression law | Known-truth strong prior | The actual declared progression family/parameters in the controlled simulator | Primary source for valid/corrupt labels. Verify that required metadata are recoverable before EXP-006. |
| Paris crack growth | Conditional strong prior | Stage-II crack propagation when a defensible crack/damage mapping and stress-intensity evidence exist | A Paris-shaped curve on normalized RUL is a surrogate, not direct crack measurement. Treat parameters and mapping as uncertain. |
| Palmgren-Miner/L10 bearing life | Population/statistical prior | Load-life accumulation under stated assumptions | It is not a deterministic individual RUL law. PRONOSTIA documentation warns that theoretical bearing-life and characteristic-frequency calculations do not reproduce its observed failures. |
| Temperature/lubrication modifier | Conditional multiphysics prior | Change in viscosity/life modifier where temperature, lubricant properties, load, and speed are available or bounded | Use an availability mask. The one-run vibration/temperature dataset can support a mechanism case study but not cross-bearing generalization. |

The present Strong-PINN embeds Paris, contact-pressure, temperature-viscosity,
contamination, L10/aSKF, and Miner structure. On IMS it relies on assumed rather than measured
crack, pressure, capacity, viscosity, and contamination values. Those residuals are a
misspecified-prior test case; they must no longer be described collectively as verified
“actual physics.”

## 5. Dataset roles and locked leakage controls

### 5.1 Controlled synthetic benchmark

**Bearings with Varying Degradation Behaviors v2** is the primary known-truth test bed. It
contains 28 training and 12 test run-to-failure trajectories generated under varied load,
life, dynamics, noise, and degradation families.

Before modeling, EXP-006 must determine exactly which trajectory-level generator parameters
and progression-family labels can be recovered from `Data.mat` and the supplied documentation.
If law/parameter identity is not recoverable, the dataset may still be used for RUL, but it
cannot support a claimed known-truth credibility endpoint. In that case, create a transparent,
open Python controlled-degradation generator from published equations rather than reverse-
engineering or modifying the supplied raw data.

Preserve the official train/test design. Fit preprocessing and credibility calibration on the
training trajectories only. Deliberate stress regimes will include, where supported:

- correct law and parameters;
- correct law with parameter uncertainty;
- biased load/life parameters;
- wrong degradation-family assignment;
- omitted temperature/load modifier;
- spurious modifier when its required sensor is absent;
- increased observation noise; and
- operation/distribution shift without a change in the governing law.

The last two distinguish “hard prediction” from “invalid physics.”

### 5.2 Primary real benchmark

**PRONOSTIA/FEMTO** is the primary real cross-condition benchmark because it provides complete
accelerated run-to-failure bearing trajectories under three load/speed conditions. Preserve
the official learning/test roles. Validation must use whole learning-set bearings, and final
testing must use the isolated official test bearings with ground-truth endpoints recovered
from the full-test archive only for evaluation.

No windows from one bearing may cross train, calibration, validation, or test partitions.
No parameter, scaler, feature selector, early stopping rule, credibility threshold, or
conformal quantile may use test-bearing labels.

### 5.3 External replication

**IMS** remains an external real-data replication using the frozen four held-out-trajectory
folds from Runs 4 and 5. These only contain four observed failures, so bearing is the unit of
analysis and claims must acknowledge the small sample.

### 5.4 Mechanism case study

**Ball Bearing Vibration and Temperature Run-to-Failure** may be used to test temperature and
lubrication evidence. Because it contains one run, it cannot establish cross-bearing accuracy,
domain generalization, or statistical superiority.

### 5.5 Excluded from direct RUL evidence

**CWRU** and its processed derivatives are seeded-fault diagnostic datasets. They may support
representation pretraining only if separately justified, but are excluded from direct
run-to-failure RUL results.

## 6. Prediction-time information and representations

All compared models must receive equivalent causal information at time (t). Bidirectional
processing is permitted only within a window ending at (t); no samples after (t) may enter
the prediction. Results using a full future sequence must be labeled offline smoothing and
cannot be compared as online RUL prediction.

The primary method comparison will use one frozen feature-based representation so the effect
of physics credibility is identifiable. After the method and hyperparameters are frozen, one
confirmatory representation experiment will compare:

- feature-only;
- raw vibration windows; and
- a hybrid raw plus physically defined spectral/statistical representation.

Representation selection uses training/validation only. Every physics method and baseline in
the primary comparison uses the same selected representation, target, and test population.

## 7. Required baselines

At minimum, compare:

1. the identical data-only causal backbone;
2. Weak-PINN with monotonicity/boundary priors;
3. fixed Strong-PINN from Runs 4/5;
4. validation-selected scalar physics weight;
5. ReLoBRaLo or another clearly attributed generic adaptive multi-loss baseline;
6. adaptive Paris regularization following Dhibi et al. where its assumptions are applicable;
7. AttnPINN/DeepHPM as a latent-physics reference;
8. a representative domain-generalization baseline on the real cross-domain study;
9. PriorCred-RUL; and
10. synthetic-only oracle, anti-oracle, all-on, all-off, and random credibility controls.

If a published baseline cannot be faithfully reproduced because code, details, license, or
data are unavailable, label the implementation as an adaptation and document the difference.
Do not imply official author code was used when it was not.

## 8. Outcomes

### 8.1 Primary outcomes

- Physics-validity AUROC and AUPRC on held-out synthetic regimes.
- Brier score and expected calibration error for credibility.
- Positive physics regret, reported per fold and as a hierarchical aggregate.
- Equal-bearing macro RMSE in original RUL units.
- Real-data non-inferiority to the identical data-only backbone.

### 8.2 Secondary outcomes

- MAE, MSE, normalized RMSE, R-squared, and declared PHM/RUL score.
- Early-, middle-, and late-life errors.
- Worst-bearing error and between-bearing dispersion.
- Spearman association between label-free credibility and realized physics regret.
- Failure/fallback rate and credibility by prior, bearing, condition, and lifecycle region.
- Physics residuals, parameter recovery, and identifiability on synthetic data.
- Parameter count, training time, inference time, convergence, and numerical failures.
- Predictive interval coverage, mean interval width, interval score, and conditional coverage
  by bearing, operating condition, and lifecycle region when UQ is enabled.

Low assumed-law residual is never sufficient evidence of valid physics. Report it alongside
parameter recovery, validity labels where available, and predictive regret.

## 9. Statistical analysis

- Use at least five common random seeds for the final comparison.
- Treat complete bearings/trajectories, not windows, as independent sampling units.
- Report per-bearing results, macro means, seed means/standard deviations, and 95% hierarchical
  bootstrap confidence intervals resampling bearings first and seeds second.
- Use paired comparisons because all methods share the same folds and seeds.
- Correct the family of primary pairwise comparisons with Holm's method.
- Report effect sizes and confidence intervals; do not rely only on a p-value.
- Keep PRONOSTIA, IMS, and synthetic estimates separate before any hierarchical synthesis.
- Predeclare exclusions and numerical-failure handling. Failed models remain in the table.
- Freeze model selection before final test evaluation. The test set is evaluated once per
  locked final method/seed, not during method development.

## 10. Success and stop criteria

The central hypothesis is supported only if all of the following hold:

1. Synthetic validity discrimination has AUROC at least 0.80 and a 95% interval lower bound
   above 0.50 on held-out corruption/law regimes.
2. Credibility is not collapsed: neither the all-on nor all-off rate exceeds 90% without a
   predeclared dataset-specific physical explanation.
3. PriorCred-RUL reduces aggregate positive physics regret by at least 30% relative to fixed
   Strong-PINN and improves it relative to the generic adaptive-weight baseline.
4. On each primary real dataset, PriorCred-RUL is non-inferior to the identical data-only
   backbone within a 2% macro-RMSE margin, while improving over fixed Strong-PINN in the
   hierarchical paired comparison.
5. No leakage, future context, inconsistent test population, or test-driven tuning is found.

If criterion 1 fails, do not proceed to claim credibility-guided physics. If criterion 1
passes but 3/4 fail, publishable value may remain as a negative study of prior diagnostics,
but not as an improved RUL method. Claims of state-of-the-art accuracy require separate,
fair benchmark evidence and are not part of these minimum criteria.

## 11. Mandatory ablations

- Remove each credibility evidence source individually.
- Fixed versus time-varying versus trajectory-level credibility.
- Frozen/cross-fitted estimator versus end-to-end gate.
- Stop-gradient versus unrestricted gradient.
- One prior at a time and multi-prior combination.
- Correct, uncertain, and corrupted parameters under the same law.
- Law mismatch versus ordinary covariate shift.
- Data-only, weak-prior fallback, fixed strong physics, and oracle selection.
- Feature-only versus the one frozen raw/hybrid confirmation.
- With and without predictive UQ; do not conflate prediction uncertainty with prior validity.

## 12. Reproducibility and artifacts

Every experiment follows the repository `AGENTS.md` contract. Additionally save:

- per-sample/per-prior credibility and availability mask;
- credibility feature values and fitted calibration parameters;
- synthetic validity/corruption labels and generator identity;
- prior equations, units, assumed parameters, identified parameters, and uncertainty;
- per-prior raw and weighted residual histories;
- fallback decisions and reasons;
- physics-regret tables; and
- all primary statistical-analysis inputs and bootstrap seeds.

## 13. Locked boundaries before implementation

The following may not be changed silently:

- the central applicability-versus-weighting distinction;
- synthetic known-truth validation before real-data claims;
- complete-bearing splits and causal information;
- the identical data-only fallback/backbone;
- the primary outcomes and success criteria;
- the five-seed final comparison; or
- the requirement to report failed and negative-transfer cases.

Any change requires a dated protocol amendment stating why it was made, whether any test
results had already been seen, and which claims are affected.
